Common Conditions

Understanding Pediatric GI Conditions

Crohn's Disease & IBD

Inflammatory bowel diseases causing chronic inflammation of the GI tract. Management includes medication, nutrition, and regular monitoring.

Acid Reflux (GERD)

Gastroesophageal reflux disease occurs when stomach acid flows back into the esophagus. Common in infants and older children alike.

Sucrose Intolerance

A rare digestive condition where the body cannot properly digest sucrose. Enzyme replacement therapy (Sucraid) and dietary changes are effective treatments.

Liver Disease

Pediatric liver conditions include fatty liver disease, hepatitis, elevated enzymes, and cholestasis. Early detection is key to effective management.

Functional Abdominal Pain

Recurrent or chronic abdominal pain without a clear structural or biochemical cause. Assessment often includes dietary and behavioral strategies.

Nutritional Disorders

Failure to thrive, malnutrition, and vitamin/mineral deficiencies are evaluated and treated through dietary plans tailored to your child's needs.
